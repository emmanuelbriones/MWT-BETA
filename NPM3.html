    What is Transportation Performance Management?

    Between 2016 and 2017, the Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) and the Federal Transit Administration (FTA) published several rules establishing performance measures and reporting requirements for State Departments of Transportation (DOTs), Metropolitan Planning Organizations (MPOs), and transit agencies. Among other changes, the rules require State DOTs and MPOs to take a performance-based approach to planning for mobility needs and programming transportation funding.

    PM 3 - System Performance

    PM 3-1 Percent of the person-miles traveled on the Interstate that are reliable
    (IH System)

    Methodology Show Graphs

    For § 490.307(a)(1) the measure for percentage of lane-miles of the Interstate System in Good condition shall be computed to the one tenth of a percent as follows (23 CFR 490.313(f)(2)):
    $$\text{Percentage of Pavements on the Interestate System in Good Condition} = 100 \times \frac{\sum_{g=1}[(End Point - BeginPoint) \times Through Lanes]section_g}{\sum_{t=1}[(End Point - BeginPoint) \times Through Lanes]section_t} $$

    Definitions

    Good = total number of mainline highway Interstate System sections where the overall condition is Good.
    g = a section's overall condition is determined Good per paragraphs (b) or (c) of section § 490.313
    t = an Interstate System section
    Total = total number of mainline highway Interstate System sections excluding bridges, unpaved surface and “other” surface types, and missing data sections, described in paragraph (f)(1) and (b)(4)(i) of section § 490.313
    Begin_Point = Begin Milepost of each section g or t
    End Point = End Milepost of each section g or t; and
    Through_lanes = the number of lanes designated for through-traffic represented by a section g or t.
    Two separate sets of targets are provided for Texas and New Mexico, as per § 490.209(d)(2)
