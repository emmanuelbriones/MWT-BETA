    What is Transportation Performance Management?

    Between 2016 and 2017, the Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) and the Federal Transit Administration (FTA) published several rules establishing performance measures and reporting requirements for State Departments of Transportation (DOTs), Metropolitan Planning Organizations (MPOs), and transit agencies. Among other changes, the rules require State DOTs and MPOs to take a performance-based approach to planning for mobility needs and programming transportation funding.

    PM 2 - Infrastructure

    Infrastructure performance is measured by six performance measures. The Bridge and Pavement Performance Management (PM 2) Final Rule, which is codified in 23 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) Part 490 defines the following national performance measures for pavement and bridges.

    PM 2-1 Percentage of Pavements on the Interstate System in Good Condition

    Methodology Show Graphs

    For § 490.307(a)(1) the measure for percentage of lane-miles of the Interstate System in Good condition shall be computed to the one tenth of a percent as follows (23 CFR 490.313(f)(2)):
    $$\text{Percentage of Pavements on the Interestate System in Good Condition} = 100 \times \frac{\sum_{g=1}[(End Point - BeginPoint) \times Through Lanes]section_g}{\sum_{t=1}[(End Point - BeginPoint) \times Through Lanes]section_t} $$

    Definitions

    Good = total number of mainline highway Interstate System sections where the overall condition is Good.
    g = a section's overall condition is determined Good per paragraphs (b) or (c) of section § 490.313
    t = an Interstate System section
    Total = total number of mainline highway Interstate System sections excluding bridges, unpaved surface and “other” surface types, and missing data sections, described in paragraph (f)(1) and (b)(4)(i) of section § 490.313
    Begin_Point = Begin Milepost of each section g or t
    End Point = End Milepost of each section g or t; and
    Through_lanes = the number of lanes designated for through-traffic represented by a section g or t.
    Two separate sets of targets are provided for Texas and New Mexico, as per § 490.209(d)(2)

    PM 2-5 Percentage of NHS Bridges classified as in Good Condition

    Methodology Show Graphs

    The performance measure for the percentage of NHS bridges classified as in Good condition shall be calculated as per § 490.409. The bridges carrying the NHS which includes on- and off-ramps connected to the NHS will be classified as Good based on the following criterion (§ 23 CFR 490.409(b)(1)): When the lowest rating of the 3 NBI items for a bridge (Items 58 - Deck, 59 - Superstructure, 60 - Substructure) is 7, 8, or 9, the bridge will be classified as Good. When the rating of NBI item for a culvert (Item 62 - Culverts) is 7, 8, or 9, the culvert will be classified as Good. Two separate sets of targets are provided for Texas and New Mexico, as per § 490.209(d)(2).
    For § 490.407(c)(1), the measure for the percentage of bridges classified as in Good condition shall be computed and reported to the one tenth of a percent as follows (§ 23 CFR 490.409(c)(1)): $$\text{Percentage of Bridges Classified in Good Condition} = 100 \times \frac{\sum_{g=1}[Length \times Width]Bridge_g}{\sum_{s=1}[Length \times Width]Bridge_s} $$ The measures identified in § 490.407(c) shall be used to establish targets in accordance with § 490.105 and report targets and conditions described in § 490.107.

    Definitions

    - GOOD = total number of the applicable bridges, where their condition is Good per paragraph (b)(1) of this section.
    - g = a bridge determined to be in Good condition per paragraph (b)(1) of this section; - Length = corresponding value of NBI Item 49 - Structure Length for every applicable bridge
    - Width = corresponding value of NBI Item 52 - Deck Width or value of Item 32 Approach Roadway Width for culverts where the roadway is on a fill [i.e., traffic does not directly run on the top slab (or wearing surface) of the culvert] and the headwalls do not affect the flow of traffic for every applicable bridge
    - s = an applicable bridge per paragraph (b) of this section.
    - TOTAL = total number of the applicable bridges specified in paragraph (b) of this section.

    PM 2-6 Percentage of NHS Bridges classified as in Poor Condition

    Methodology Show Graphs

    The performance measure for the percentage of NHS bridges classified as in Poor condition shall be calculated as per § 490.409. The bridges carrying the NHS which includes on- and off-ramps connected to the NHS will be classified as Poor based on the following criterion (§ 23 CFR 490.409(b)(3)) : When the lowest rating of the 3 NBI items for a bridge is 4, 3, 2, 1, or 0, the bridge will be classified as Poor. When the rating of NBI item for a culvert is 4, 3, 2, 1, or 0, the culvert will be classified as Poor.
    For § 490.407(c)(2), the measure for the percentage of bridges classified as in Poor condition shall be computed and reported to the one tenth of a percent as follows (§ 23 CFR 490.409(c)(2)): $$\text{Percentage of Bridges Classified in Poor Condition} = 100 \times \frac{\sum_{p=1}[Length \times Width]Bridge_p}{\sum_{s=1}[Length \times Width]Bridge_s} $$ The measures identified in § 490.407(c) shall be used to establish targets in accordance with § 490.105 and report targets and conditions described in § 490.107.

    Definitions

    Poor = total number of the applicable bridges, where their condition is Poor per paragraph (b)(3) of this section.
    p = a bridge determined to be in Poor condition per paragraph (b)(3) of this section.
    Length = corresponding value of NBI Item 49 - Structure Length for every applicable bridge.
    Width = corresponding value of NBI Item 52 - Deck Width or value of Item 32 Approach Roadway Width for culverts where the roadway is on a fill [i.e., traffic does not directly run on the top slab (or wearing surface) of the culvert] and the headwalls do not affect the flow of traffic for every applicable bridge. s = an applicable bridge per paragraph (b) of this section.
    TOTAL = total number of the applicable bridges specified in paragraph (b) of this section.
